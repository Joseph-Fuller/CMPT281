A Brief History

  1. Early Years

    Joseph Alex Fuller was born July 19, 1996 in Saskatoon, Saskatchewan. He grew up in a house in the north end of the city with his parents, brother, and a dog named Jake. Joseph went to school from kindergarden to Grade 8 at Sister O'Brien school, where he attended the French immersion program offered there. Around this time, he also started taking piano lessons, which he continued to take until his sophomore year of high school. Although he continued to enroll in the immersion program at Bishop James Mahoney high school, Joseph cannot recall a single instance where his additional language skills have had a practical benefit. While attending high school, Joseph acquired an interest in music and music production while playing in BJM's concert band, where he played trombone. During this time, he also worked as a merchandise clerk for a now-defunct chain of grocery stores. Overall, this proved to be a tedious yet character-building exercise in discipline.

  2. Continuing Education

    After graduation, he enrolled in the Media Arts Production program at Saskatchewan Polytechnic (formerly SIAST). This program, only being offered at the institute's Woodland campus, necessitated a move to Prince Albert, Saskatchewan. This would be Joseph's first time living on his own. He enjoyed the independence despite the conditions in which it was experienced. Prince Albert isn't a great place for young people. The course Joseph was attending also came with its own mixed merits. It became obvious that it ws the product of a bygone era; still looking at digital media as a novelty. This proved to be in contrast with the realities of the industry, and the program seemed to lag behind by several years. Nevertheless, Joseph managed to secure a job at a Saskatoon-based live-events production company upon completing his 8 month certificate.

  3. The "Real" World

    The production company proved to be an interesting introduction to the realm of full-time employment and an education of its own kind. Over an 18 month period, Joseph learned a great deal about the world of entertainment and the people who occupy it. Working with a team under tough conditions proved to be immensely satisfying: overcoming obstacles and solving problems really brings people together. Unfortunately the management structure of the company began to wear on him and ultimately proved psychologically draining. As much as he had hoped to continue in the industry, he decided to leave and continue his education.

  4. Future

    This is where we find Joseph Fuller today: starting his second full semester at the U of S. Resilient and hopeful for a future in an industry in which he can progress. Believing in a better tomorrow and working to make it happen.
